Mental Health Care: Prioritizing Your Cognitive and Emotional Well-Being

At Garcia Family Medicine, we understand that mental health is just as important as physical health. Mental health care involves addressing cognitive, social, and emotional well-being, ensuring that individuals can manage stress, connect with others, and maintain a balanced life. Mental health conditionsâ€”such as ADHD, ADD, depression, anxiety, and bipolar disorderâ€”are common but often overlooked.

Our mental health care services are designed to provide ongoing support, helping individuals manage long-term mental health conditions in a compassionate and personalized manner. What Is Mental Health Care?

Mental health care focuses on maintaining and improving the psychological, emotional, and social well-being of individuals. Mental health conditions can range from mild anxiety and depression to more complex disorders such as bipolar disorder and obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). Mental health impacts how we think, feel, and act, and can affect every aspect of life, from relationships to work performance.

Mental health problems are more common than many people realize. In fact, millions of individuals around the world struggle with various mental health issues, yet many go undiagnosed or untreated due to the stigma surrounding mental health or the complexities of accessing affordable care. This is why Dr. Garcia keeps a sensitive and receptive ear open at every patient encounter for signs and symptoms of mental health concerns that are missed or misinterpreted by other providers. Often patients are afraid or embarrassed to come out and state that they have a mental health concern, making it important to recognize those calls for help that may be masked by physical symptoms. But she is just as careful not to assign a mental health diagnosis to physical symptoms that may require a more intensive medical evaluation.

The patient who is seeking mental health care does not have to sign up for a special "mental health plan" at Garcia Family Medicine. As stated above, it is part of our primary care membership. The patient will not be told that they need to seek a psychiatrist or therapist to receive care. Evaluation, diagnosis, and treatment are provided by Dr. Garcia. Prescription and management of mental health medicines are also part of the primary care membership. The benefits of the direct primary care model roll into our mental health care, providing more time with Dr. Garcia for evaluation and closer follow-up, whether you are started on medicines or not, than can be expected in a traditional, insurance-based model.

The Role of Counseling in Mental Health Care

Some patients will do well with simple acknowledgment of a mental health issue and the part that it plays in their physical symptoms. Other patients will require medication and management of the medication in treatment of their mental health concern. There are some patients, however, who want or will require counseling. At Garcia Family Medicine, we do not refer you to a therapist; rather, Dr. Garcia provides counseling that is patient-specific and tailored to your individual needs. You may receive counseling in addition to medication prescription and management or counseling alone as the sole treatment for your mental health concerns. This is available to you for an additional counseling fee ($85 per hour), a fee that is amazingly competitive compared to traditional counseling. She also provides couples counseling.

Dr. Garcia's philosophy of counseling is unique, compassionate, and direct. It cannot be listed as any particular style but is something that she has developed over years of medical practice by working with all her patients, both those with medical concerns only and those who are specifically seeking mental health treatment. She has learned that everyone has at least one point in their lives when they are in need of mental health support from someone who will listen with wisdom and understanding, who neither has an agenda nor a particular "style" or format into which the patient must fit. The patient is empowered to advocate for themselves, working with Dr. Garcia to discover the direction their counseling should go.

The Role of Direct Primary Care (DPC) in Mental Health Care

The Direct Primary Care (DPC) model plays a vital role in improving access to mental health services by offering an affordable, patient-centered approach to care. In the traditional insurance-based model, mental health care is often expensive, complicated by co-pays, deductibles, referrals, and limited access to providers. Many patients experience long wait times before receiving treatment, limited time with their doctors, or annual limits on how many counseling sessions they may have, all of which hinder effective treatment. For the patient with NO insurance, the costs are even higher since insurance companies negotiate lower prices with medical and mental health practices and only pay a percentage of that lower price, leaving the patient paying their part of that percentage. The only people who pay full price in traditional medical practices are the patients with no insurance.

With DPC, patients pay a monthly membership fee that covers primary care services, which includes mental health care. This means that patients have unlimited access to Dr. Tess without the need for co-pays or deductibles. There is an additional fee per session ($85 for one hour) for counseling. At Garcia Family Medicine, we prioritize the doctor-patient relationship, ensuring that individuals receive the time, attention, and personalized care they need to address their medical and mental health concerns.

Value of Time Spent with the Doctor in DPC vs. Traditional Insurance

One of the most significant advantages of the Direct Primary Care model is the time that patients are able to spend with Dr. Garcia. In traditional insurance-based practices, doctors are often required to see a high volume of patients each day, leading to rushed appointments that may last only 10-15 minutes. This can make it difficult for patients to thoroughly discuss their mental health concerns or receive comprehensive care.

In contrast, DPC allows for longer, more personalized visits. Doctors in DPC practices typically have smaller patient panels, which means they can spend more time with each individual patient. Appointments (regular appointments, not counseling) often last 30 minutes to an hour, giving patients ample time to discuss their mental health issues, explore treatment options, and receive the care they need. This additional time is especially important for mental health care, as many conditions require a nuanced understanding of the patient's emotional and psychological well-being. With DPC, Dr. Garcia is able to build stronger relationships with her patients, make more accurate diagnoses, and develop more appropriate plans of care.

DPC emphasizes whole-person care, which means that Dr. Garcia takes the time to understand all aspects of a patient's healthâ€”physical, emotional, and mental. This is why mental health is part of our regular primary care membership plan. For individuals dealing with mental health issues, this approach ensures that they receive comprehensive care that addresses all facets of their well-being.

Mental health care is essential for living a balanced and fulfilling life. What is an Independent Medical Evaluation (IME)?

An Independent Medical Evaluation is a comprehensive medical examination performed by an independent physician to provide an objective assessment of an individual's medical condition, disability status, or treatment needs. IMEs are often requested by insurance companies, employers, attorneys, or courts to obtain an unbiased medical opinion.

Common Reasons for IMEs:

  • Workers' Compensation Claims: Evaluation of work-related injuries or illnesses
  • Disability Determinations: Assessment for short-term or long-term disability benefits
  • Personal Injury Cases: Medical evaluation for auto accidents or other injury claims
  • Return to Work Assessments: Determining work capacity and restrictions
  • Treatment Appropriateness: Evaluating proposed medical treatments
  • Maximum Medical Improvement: Determining if further treatment will provide benefit

The IME Process:

  • Medical Records Review: Thorough examination of all relevant medical records
  • Comprehensive Physical Examination: Detailed assessment by Dr. Garcia
  • Medical History Review: Discussion of symptoms, treatment history, and functional limitations
  • Diagnostic Test Review: Analysis of lab work, imaging, and other test results
  • Professional Report: Detailed written report with medical opinions and recommendations

What's Included in the IME Report:

  • Summary of medical records reviewed
  • Patient history and chief complaints
  • Findings from physical examination
  • Review of diagnostic studies
  • Medical opinions on diagnosis and causation
  • Assessment of current functional status
  • Work capacity and restrictions (if applicable)
  • Treatment recommendations
  • Prognosis and need for future care

Dr. Garcia's Qualifications:

As a board-certified Family Medicine physician, Dr. Garcia brings extensive experience in evaluating a wide range of medical conditions. Her training and clinical experience enable her to provide thorough, objective medical evaluations that are well-documented and professionally presented.

Quick Turnaround:

We understand that IME reports are often time-sensitive. Dr. Garcia prioritizes timely completion of reports, typically delivering comprehensive written evaluations within 7-10 business days of the examination.

Professional and Objective:

Dr. Garcia maintains strict independence and objectivity in all IME evaluations. Her goal is to provide accurate, evidence-based medical opinions that assist in fair decision-making, regardless of who requested the evaluation.

DOT Physicals

Certified DOT physical examinations for commercial drivers. Dr. Garcia is a certified medical examiner providing thorough, efficient DOT physicals to keep you on the road.

What is a DOT Physical?

A DOT (Department of Transportation) physical is a health examination mandated by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) for commercial motor vehicle (CMV) drivers. This exam ensures that drivers are physically, mentally, and emotionally fit to operate a commercial vehicle safely.

Who Needs a DOT Physical?

  • Commercial truck drivers (CDL holders)
  • Bus drivers
  • Drivers of vehicles designed to transport 16+ passengers
  • Drivers of vehicles over 10,000 lbs GVWR
  • Drivers transporting hazardous materials requiring placards

Dr. Garcia is FMCSA Certified:

Dr. Garcia is listed on the FMCSA National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners, which means she has completed specialized training and testing to perform DOT physical examinations. This certification is required to issue valid Medical Examiner's Certificates.

What's Included in the DOT Physical:

  • Medical History Review: Complete health history and current medications
  • Vision Testing: Visual acuity and peripheral vision screening
  • Hearing Test: Ability to perceive forced whisper at specific distances
  • Blood Pressure Measurement: Screening for hypertension
  • Urinalysis: Testing for underlying medical conditions
  • Physical Examination: Assessment of overall health and physical fitness
  • Medical Examiner's Certificate: Issued immediately if qualified

Examination Components:

  • General appearance and skin condition
  • Eyes (visual acuity, color vision)
  • Ears (hearing test)
  • Mouth and throat
  • Heart and cardiovascular system
  • Lungs and chest
  • Abdomen and digestive system
  • Spine and extremities
  • Neurological function
  • Mental and emotional health

Certification Periods:

Based on your health status, Dr. Garcia may certify you for:

  • 2 Years: For drivers in excellent health
  • 1 Year: For certain controlled conditions requiring monitoring
  • 3-6 Months: For temporary conditions requiring follow-up

Same-Day Appointments Available:

We understand that truckers need to get back on the road quickly. We offer same-day DOT physical appointments when available, and most examinations can be completed in 30-45 minutes.

What to Bring:

  • Current driver's license
  • List of current medications
  • Glasses or contacts (if you wear them)
  • Hearing aids (if you use them)
  • Medical records for any chronic conditions
