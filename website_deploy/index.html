CoreLift Pelvic Health

Revolutionary non-invasive electromagnetic therapy for pelvic floor strengthening and bladder control. FDA-cleared EMSELLA technology treats stress and urge incontinence in comfortable 28-minute sessions.

What is CoreLift?

CoreLift uses the FDA-cleared BTL EMSELLA chair, which delivers high-intensity focused electromagnetic (HIFEM) technology to strengthen your pelvic floor muscles. You remain fully clothed while sitting comfortably in the chair during your 28-minute treatment session.

How It Works:

The EMSELLA chair causes thousands of supramaximal pelvic floor muscle contractions in a single session‚Äîthe equivalent of doing thousands of Kegel exercises. These deep pelvic floor contractions restore neuromuscular control and strengthen the muscles that support your bladder and pelvic organs.

Conditions Treated:

  • Stress Incontinence: Leakage when coughing, sneezing, laughing, or exercising
  • Urge Incontinence: Sudden, strong urge to urinate with leakage
  • Pelvic Floor Weakness: Following childbirth or due to aging
  • Sexual Dysfunction: Improved pelvic muscle tone can enhance intimacy

Treatment Protocol:

  • Recommended series: 6 sessions over 3 weeks
  • Each session: 28 minutes
  • Fully clothed, non-invasive treatment
  • No downtime or recovery needed
  • Most patients see improvement after 3-4 sessions
  • Maintenance treatments every 6-12 months

Results You Can Expect:

Clinical studies show that 95% of patients report significant improvement in their quality of life. Many patients experience a dramatic reduction in pad usage, with some no longer needing pads at all. Results typically improve over 2-4 weeks following your treatment series.

Mental Health Care

Prioritizing your cognitive and emotional well-being. Mental health is just as important as physical health, and at Garcia Family Medicine, mental health care is part of our regular primary care membership.

What Is Mental Health Care?

Mental health care focuses on maintaining and improving the psychological, emotional, and social well-being of individuals. Mental health conditions can range from mild anxiety and depression to more complex disorders such as bipolar disorder and obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). Mental health impacts how we think, feel, and act, and can affect every aspect of life, from relationships to work performance.

Integrated Into Your Primary Care:

The patient seeking mental health care does not have to sign up for a special "mental health plan" at Garcia Family Medicine. Mental health is part of our primary care membership. You will not be told that you need to seek a psychiatrist or therapist to receive care. Evaluation, diagnosis, and treatment are provided by Dr. Garcia. Prescription and management of mental health medicines are also part of the primary care membership.

Conditions We Treat:

  • ADHD and ADD: Attention deficit disorders in children and adults
  • Depression: Persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest
  • Anxiety Disorders: Generalized anxiety, social anxiety, panic disorder
  • Bipolar Disorder: Mood swings including emotional highs and lows
  • PTSD: Post-traumatic stress disorder and trauma recovery
  • OCD: Obsessive-compulsive disorder

Dr. Garcia's Philosophy of Counseling:

The patient is empowered to advocate for themselves, working with Dr. Garcia to discover the direction their counseling should go.

Weight Management

Achieving sustainable, long-term health through comprehensive physician-supervised weight management. This is not a quick fix‚Äîit's a commitment to lasting change.

Let Us Be Clear:

If you are looking for a 3-month program in which you starve yourself and use a GLP-1a to quickly take off pounds to get you into your bikini for the summer or fit into your wedding dress in time for the big day, our program is not for you. It takes time to change your eating habits so that once you come off the program, you will be able to maintain your weight loss. Our weight management program is for people who are committed to change.

What Is Our Weight Management Program?

Weight management at Garcia Family Medicine involves more than just losing pounds; it's about creating sustainable, healthy behaviors that reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension and allow the patient to come off the program without experiencing weight regain.

The First Appointment:

  • Body composition analysis
  • Vital signs assessment
  • In-depth medical history
  • Thorough physical exam by Dr. Tess
  • Comprehensive lab panel to detect metabolic causes and comorbidities
  • Follow-up in 2 weeks to learn results and begin counseling

Program Components:

  • Biweekly Counseling with Dr. Tess: Understanding your relationship with food and emotional eating patterns
  • Biweekly Coaching with Gigi: Implementing changes and maintaining motivation
  • Nutrition Education: Learn what to eat and when‚Äînot about portion control or starvation
  • Exercise Program: Personalized to your fitness level and goals
  • Cyanocobalamin Injections: Fat-burning B12 injections to enhance metabolism
  • GLP-1a Agonists: Tirzepatide (with NAD precursors) when medically appropriate
  • 24/7 Support: Dr. Tess and Gigi available by text or phone to support you through challenges

About Tirzepatide:

Tirzepatide is more than just a GLP-1a agonist; it's also a GIP agonist. These hormones work in the GI system to make digestion slower, but also in the brain to decrease your appetite. Dr. Tess thoroughly evaluates each patient's eligibility before prescribing tirzepatide, ensuring that the treatment is safe, effective, and appropriate for their weight loss journey. You will not walk into the first appointment and walk out with tirzepatide‚Äîmedical evaluation comes first.

Program Timeline:

Our weight management program is not a 3-month program or even a 6-month program. If you are using tirzepatide, it takes a month to taper you from one dose to the next. The need to increase the dose depends on your response to each level. Remember that it took you time to gain that weight and it will take you time to lose it. Rapid weight loss is by itself unhealthy and sets you up for rapid regain.

Maintaining Your Weight Loss:

Once you reach your target weight, it will take some months to taper you off of the tirzepatide. During that time, we will be focusing on maintaining healthy eating at a level that you will be able to maintain once you leave the program. Our program is designed not just to help patients lose weight but to sustain that loss.

Why Choose Dr. Tess?

Dr. Tess is a board-certified Obesity Medicine Specialist who has proven the program's effectiveness to herself by losing 70 pounds‚Äîwithout Tirzepatide, but with the coaching help of Gigi who lost 70 pounds with her. Knowing ourselves how important this is, we hold your hand every step of the way.

Compassion and Acceptance:

We believe that any weight management program that perpetuates feelings of shame or failure should not be in business. Compassion and acceptance are major parts of our weight management program. We do not allow you to be alone on your weight loss journey.

Independent Medical Evaluations

Professional, objective medical assessments for legal and insurance purposes. Dr. Garcia provides comprehensive evaluations with detailed written reports.

What is an Independent Medical Evaluation (IME)?

An Independent Medical Evaluation is a comprehensive medical examination performed by an independent physician to provide an objective assessment of an individual's medical condition, disability status, or treatment needs. IMEs are often requested by insurance companies, employers, attorneys, or courts to obtain an unbiased medical opinion.

Common Reasons for IMEs:

  • Workers' Compensation Claims: Evaluation of work-related injuries or illnesses
  • Disability Determinations: Assessment for short-term or long-term disability benefits
  • Personal Injury Cases: Medical evaluation for auto accidents or other injury claims
  • Return to Work Assessments: Determining work capacity and restrictions
  • Treatment Appropriateness: Evaluating proposed medical treatments
  • Maximum Medical Improvement: Determining if further treatment will provide benefit

The IME Process:

  • Medical Records Review: Thorough examination of all relevant medical records
  • Comprehensive Physical Examination: Detailed assessment by Dr. Garcia
  • Medical History Review: Discussion of symptoms, treatment history, and functional limitations
  • Diagnostic Test Review: Analysis of lab work, imaging, and other test results
  • Professional Report: Detailed written report with medical opinions and recommendations

What's Included in the IME Report:

  • Summary of medical records reviewed
  • Patient history and chief complaints
  • Findings from physical examination
  • Review of diagnostic studies
  • Medical opinions on diagnosis and causation
  • Assessment of current functional status
  • Work capacity and restrictions (if applicable)
  • Treatment recommendations
  • Prognosis and need for future care

Dr. Garcia's Qualifications:

As a board-certified Family Medicine physician, Dr. Garcia brings extensive experience in evaluating a wide range of medical conditions. Her training and clinical experience enable her to provide thorough, objective medical evaluations that are well-documented and professionally presented.

Quick Turnaround:

We understand that IME reports are often time-sensitive. Dr. Garcia prioritizes timely completion of reports, typically delivering comprehensive written evaluations within 7-10 business days of the examination.

Professional and Objective:

Dr. Garcia maintains strict independence and objectivity in all IME evaluations. Her goal is to provide accurate, evidence-based medical opinions that assist in fair decision-making, regardless of who requested the evaluation.

DOT Physicals

Certified DOT physical examinations for commercial drivers. Dr. Garcia is a certified medical examiner providing thorough, efficient DOT physicals to keep you on the road.

What is a DOT Physical?

A DOT (Department of Transportation) physical is a health examination mandated by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) for commercial motor vehicle (CMV) drivers. This exam ensures that drivers are physically, mentally, and emotionally fit to operate a commercial vehicle safely.

Who Needs a DOT Physical?

  • Commercial truck drivers (CDL holders)
  • Bus drivers
  • Drivers of vehicles designed to transport 16+ passengers
  • Drivers of vehicles over 10,000 lbs GVWR
  • Drivers transporting hazardous materials requiring placards

Dr. Garcia is FMCSA Certified:

Dr. Garcia is listed on the FMCSA National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners, which means she has completed specialized training and testing to perform DOT physical examinations. This certification is required to issue valid Medical Examiner's Certificates.

What's Included in the DOT Physical:

  • Medical History Review: Complete health history and current medications
  • Vision Testing: Visual acuity and peripheral vision screening
  • Hearing Test: Ability to perceive forced whisper at specific distances
  • Blood Pressure Measurement: Screening for hypertension
  • Urinalysis: Testing for underlying medical conditions
  • Physical Examination: Assessment of overall health and physical fitness
  • Medical Examiner's Certificate: Issued immediately if qualified

Examination Components:

  • General appearance and skin condition
  • Eyes (visual acuity, color vision)
  • Ears (hearing test)
  • Mouth and throat
  • Heart and cardiovascular system
  • Lungs and chest
  • Abdomen and digestive system
  • Spine and extremities
  • Neurological function
  • Mental and emotional health

Certification Periods:

Based on your health status, Dr. Garcia may certify you for:

  • 2 Years: For drivers in excellent health
  • 1 Year: For certain controlled conditions requiring monitoring
  • 3-6 Months: For temporary conditions requiring follow-up

Same-Day Appointments Available:

We understand that truckers need to get back on the road quickly. We offer same-day DOT physical appointments when available, and most examinations can be completed in 30-45 minutes.

What to Bring:

  • Current driver's license
  • List of current medications
  • Glasses or contacts (if you wear them)
  • Hearing aids (if you use them)
  • Medical records for any chronic conditions

Convenient Blue Springs Location:

Our office is conveniently located in Blue Springs, Missouri, easily accessible from I-70 and major trucking routes through the Kansas City metro area.

What is an Independent Medical Evaluation (IME)?

An Independent Medical Evaluation is a comprehensive medical examination performed by an independent physician to provide an objective assessment of an individual's medical condition, disability status, or treatment needs. IMEs are often requested by insurance companies, employers, attorneys, or courts to obtain an unbiased medical opinion.

Common Reasons for IMEs:

  • Workers' Compensation Claims: Evaluation of work-related injuries or illnesses
  • Disability Determinations: Assessment for short-term or long-term disability benefits
  • Personal Injury Cases: Medical evaluation for auto accidents or other injury claims
  • Return to Work Assessments: Determining work capacity and restrictions
  • Treatment Appropriateness: Evaluating proposed medical treatments
  • Maximum Medical Improvement: Determining if further treatment will provide benefit

The IME Process:

  • Medical Records Review: Thorough examination of all relevant medical records
  • Comprehensive Physical Examination: Detailed assessment by Dr. Garcia
  • Medical History Review: Discussion of symptoms, treatment history, and functional limitations
  • Diagnostic Test Review: Analysis of lab work, imaging, and other test results
  • Professional Report: Detailed written report with medical opinions and recommendations

What's Included in the IME Report:

  • Summary of medical records reviewed
  • Patient history and chief complaints
  • Findings from physical examination
  • Review of diagnostic studies
  • Medical opinions on diagnosis and causation
  • Assessment of current functional status
  • Work capacity and restrictions (if applicable)
  • Treatment recommendations
  • Prognosis and need for future care

DOT Physicals

What is a DOT Physical?

A DOT (Department of Transportation) physical is a health examination mandated by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) for commercial motor vehicle (CMV) drivers. This exam ensures that drivers are physically, mentally, and emotionally fit to operate a commercial vehicle safely.

Who Needs a DOT Physical?

  • Commercial truck drivers (CDL holders)
  • Bus drivers
  • Drivers of vehicles designed to transport 16+ passengers
  • Drivers of vehicles over 10,000 lbs GVWR
  • Drivers transporting hazardous materials requiring placards

Dr. Garcia is FMCSA Certified:

Dr. Garcia is listed on the FMCSA National Registry of Certified Medical Examiners, which means she has completed specialized training and testing to perform DOT physical examinations. This certification is required to issue valid Medical Examiner's Certificates.

What's Included in the DOT Physical:

  • Medical History Review: Complete health history and current medications
  • Vision Testing: Visual acuity and peripheral vision screening
  • Hearing Test: Ability to perceive forced whisper at specific distances
  • Blood Pressure Measurement: Screening for hypertension
  • Urinalysis: Testing for underlying medical conditions
  • Physical Examination: Assessment of overall health and physical fitness
  • Medical Examiner's Certificate: Issued immediately if qualified

Examination Components:

  • General appearance and skin condition
  • Eyes (visual acuity, color vision)
  • Ears (hearing test)
  • Mouth and throat
  • Heart and cardiovascular system
  • Lungs and chest
  • Abdomen and digestive system
  • Spine and extremities
  • Neurological function
  • Mental and emotional health

Certification Periods:

Based on your health status, Dr. Garcia may certify you for:

  • 2 Years: For drivers in excellent health
  • 1 Year: For certain controlled conditions requiring monitoring
  • 3-6 Months: For temporary conditions requiring follow-up

Same-Day Appointments Available:

We understand that truckers need to get back on the road quickly. We offer same-day DOT physical appointments when available, and most examinations can be completed in 30-45 minutes.

What to Bring:

  • Current driver's license
  • List of current medications
  • Glasses or contacts (if you wear them)
  • Hearing aids (if you use them)
  • Medical records for any chronic conditions
